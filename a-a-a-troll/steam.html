About This Game

Fancy Skulls is a challenging first person shooter with procedural generation, permanent death and distinct art style.
Intense and tactical combat, unique weapon mods and items that change the way you play.


Foes different not only in visual design but also in their behavior. For example there is an enemy that will teleport every time you hit it, or enemy that leaves bombs that home on you, but you can shoot those bombs to use them against it.



Usable items and power ups, like friendly sentry, an eye that lets you transmute items, kite that lets you fly, matchbox that ignites all foes around you, and much more!


Each weapon is different from another, and every weapon can be modded with options that provide interesting game experiences like "walk backwards to get ammo", "12 hits with no misses give you money", "last two bullets in a magazine do double damage", "don't spend ammo while in the air", "shoot lightning bolts with shotgun".

Early Access Review
Fancy Skulls is a rogue-like, procedurally generated action FPS, the graphics are for the most part decent, they are simplistic and sort of remind me of mordern art, the combat is strategic and requires accuracy to survive, enemies have weak points and if you're accurate you can one-shot them. The shops are also decent, they contain a few cool upgrades and the weapons are also pretty varied. The quiet mood of this game is pretty atmospheric and the sounds when you shoot something are very.. comforting.

It's permadeath, meaning you can take 4 hits before dying and restarting over. There is a form of mana using eyeballs you can find which have different abilities and recharge over time, the main revolver you have has infinite ammo and 6 rounds, every other weapon has limited ammo and you'll have to keep finding more. There are keys which unlock chests and crates which you can break.

The game is a bit.. too hard at times, I find myself to get obliterated in some rooms because of the layout and the types of enemies, while in others I blitz through.
There are about.. maybe 7-8 possible types of enemies? Each room is procedurally generated, making you think to find the best strategy for taking them out.

So far I feel the game doesn't have enough content, it's a fun game, don't get me wrong, especially since I got it for a mere $0.50 on sale, but I doubt you'll put over 14hrs in it unless you're a hardcore rogue-like fan or just love the aspects of the game. There really isn't much to do after you play it a couple times or get annoyed and rage quit from an unfair room, I'd recommend to wait for it to go on sale, even then, 60% off at least.
